Drum, the sound of which is produced by the vibration of a stretched membrane. Drums are usually either cylindrical or bowl-shaped. The drum is a universal instrument and very ancient; a drum dating to 6000 bc has been found in Moravia.

A drum stick (or drumstick) is a type of percussion mallet used particularly for playing snare drum, drum kit, and some other percussion instruments, and particularly for playing unpitched percussion.

Cymbals are musical percussion instruments consisting of a concave metal plate fixed at a central hole and free to vibrate around its circumference. Is part of the family of idiophone instruments of indefinite pitch.

More About This

Here are some more facts about drums: Types of drums There are many types of drums, including the snare drum, timpani, bass drum, and tom-toms. The most basic drum kit configuration has two tom-toms, one high and one low. Drum shells Drum shells are made from different types of wood, which can affect the sound. For example, maple drums have a warm sound, while birch drums have a more aggressive sound. The number of wood layers, or plies, also affects the sound. Thinner plies make the drum more resonant. Drumming as exercise Drumming can be a great form of exercise, burning more calories than cycling, hiking, or weight lifting in a half hour. It can also reduce stress hormones. Drumming grips There are two main ways to hold drumsticks: the traditional grip and the matched grip. The traditional grip uses an overhand grip for the right hand and an underhand grip for the left. The matched grip has both sticks held the same way, with the palms facing down.
